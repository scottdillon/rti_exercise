One can see from our summary statistics of continuous variables that the average person from this sample is 38, almost 39 years old. They work 40 hours a week. They have not completed high school. Twenty four percent of these American persons (US citizens and permanent residents) make more than $50,000 per year.

Seventy five percent of people in this sample work 45 hours or less with a tight distribution of hours around 40 for most adults.

In the second table, I have grouped the continuous data variables by those who make over $50K, whether they are married or not, and their race.

The first thing to notice about our data is those who make more than $50K have more education than those who don't. About two years more generally.

In the group that makes less than $50K, there are comparitively fewer married census respondents than unmarried. One suspects that this is younger, unmarried people making less money and the mean, age of these groups agrees with the unmarried group who makes less than $50K being about 10 years younger on average.

In the less than $50K group, the hours worked and education levels are similar.

The over $50K group is the opposite with respect to being married and age. There are more married people who make over $50K while they are still older but less so than those who are not married. Those unmarried respondents who make more than $50K also work the longest hours and have some of the highest education levels in the sample.
